## Proposed Phase-1 baseline — "Nudge" via chamfer matching

My logic to debate with Gemini:

3. **Confidence proxies fall out natively**: (a) residual mean distance at optimum (low=good), (b) basin sharpness / peak-to-sidelobe (sharp=confident), (c) post-shift overlap. No extra model needed for raw score.

6. **Calibrate** raw confidence → [0,1] via isotonic/Platt — **needs `score()` as oracle**.

Why chamfer over alternatives: it directly encodes "shape right, location off," and its residual *is* the confidence signal — one objective serves both nudge and calibration.
